About my research

My research interests are broadly in low-dimensional topology. More specifically, I explore the existence and uniqueness of surfaces in the 4-ball bounding a given knot by examining the maps they induce on Khovanov homology.
I would like to learn more about:
  • 4-manifolds
  • Heegaard-Floer homology
  • knot concordance
  • knotted surfaces in 4-manifolds
  • anything you're working on!

Papers

Khovanov homology and exotic surfaces in the 4-ball

with Kyle Hayden
This paper shows that the cobordism maps on Khovanov homology can distinguish between exotically knotted smooth surfaces in the 4-ball that are isotopic through ambient homeomorphisms but not ambient diffeomorphisms. Our proof uses a new approach to distinguishing the cobordism maps on Khovanov homology, and we highlight additional applications of this approach.

Relative Khovanov-Jacobsson Classes

with Jonah Swann, to appear in Algebraic & Geometric Topology
In this paper, we define an invariant of the smooth boundary-preserving isotopy class of a surfaces in the 4-ball. This invariant comes from the Khovanov homology of the boundary link of the surface. Previous work showed that when the boundary link is empty, this invariant is determined by the genus of the surface. We show that this relative invariant: can obstruct sliceness of knots; detects a pair of slices for 9_46; is not hindered by detecting connected sums with knotted 2-spheres.

Multiplication operators on weighted Banach spaces of a tree

with Robert Allen, Bulletin of the Korean Mathematical Society
In this paper, we study multiplication operators on the weighted Banach spaces of an infinite tree. We characterize the bounded and the compact operators, as well as determine the operator norm. In addition, we determine the spectrum of the bounded multiplication operators and characterize the isometries. Finally, we study the multiplication operators between the weighted Banach spaces and the Lipschitz space by characterizing the bounded and the compact operators, determining estimates on the operator norm, and showing there are no isometries.

A binary algebra structure is a pair (S, *) consisting of a set S and a binary operation * on that set.

To produce an algebra structure which is not a group, a group property (associativity, identity, inverse) must be violated. Here are some examples:

  • The positive integers, rationals, and reals under addition have no identity.
  • The positive reals under multiplication have no inverses.

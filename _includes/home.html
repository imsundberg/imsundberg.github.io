About Me
I am a third year graduate student at Bryn Mawr College (as of Fall 2018), researching low-dimensional topology with my advisor Paul Melvin. My research interests are currently in smooth 4-manifolds and Khovanov homology. For more info on my research, see my Research page.
I am also interested in teaching, which has been my passion since my days as an undergraduate math tutor. While at Bryn Mawr, I have been a TA for a variety of math courses and learned many (un)successful teaching strategies. This fall (2019), I will pursue the Dean's Certificate in Pedagogy at Bryn Mawr.
Another passion of mine is working with the Bryn Mawr community. I enjoy spending time on running/attending seminars in the math department, along with serving on the executive board of the Graduate Student Association (GSA). In particular, I have focused my energies on reflecting Bryn Mawr's diversity, equity, and inclusion framework in the GSA mission and active responsibilities. For more info on my involvement, see my Involvement page.

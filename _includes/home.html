About me

My name is Isaac Sundberg; my pronouns are he/they. I am a sixth-year graduate student in mathematics at Bryn Mawr College, working with Paul Melvin. I expect to graduate next spring (2022).
My research interests are in low-dimensional topology, specifically, I use Khovanov homology to study 3 and 4-dimensional properties of surfaces bounding knots and links. A picture of one of these surfaces can be seen on my research page.
Before coming to Bryn Mawr College, I was an undergraduate at the University of Wisconsin - La Crosse where I majored in math and minored in computer science. While there, I was very fortunate to tutor math for 3 years, allowing me to discover my passion for teaching.
